Soymilk Broth Ramen

Description

Vegetarian Soymilk Broth Ramen is a creamy, umami-rich noodle soup that combines the mild sweetness of soy milk with the deep flavors of miso, garlic, and ginger. Loaded with tender ramen noodles, sautéed mushrooms, spinach, and sweet corn, this dish is a hearty and wholesome take on traditional ramen. Garnished with green onions, sesame seeds, and optional nori strips or chili oil, it offers a deliciously complex and satisfying bowl of comfort.

Ingredients

  • 2 servings fresh or dried ramen noodles
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 2 tablespoons miso paste (white or red)
  • 3 cups unsweetened soy milk
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce (or to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon rice vinegar
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms (shiitake or button)
  • 1 cup baby spinach
  • ½ cup corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
  • 2 green onions, sliced (for garnish)
  • 1 soft-boiled egg (optional for vegetarian, not vegan)
  • Optional toppings: sesame seeds, nori strips, chili oil

Steps

  1. Cook the ramen noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat sesame oil in a medium pot over medium heat. Add garlic and ginger, sautéing until fragrant (about 1 minute).
  3. Add miso paste, stirring until it dissolves in the oil. Gradually whisk in the soy milk and vegetable broth, ensuring the miso fully blends.
  4. Stir in soy sauce and rice vinegar. Bring to a gentle simmer but do not boil (this prevents the soy milk from curdling)
  5. Add mushrooms and cook for 5 minutes until tender. Stir in baby spinach and corn, cooking for 2 more minutes.
  6. Divide the cooked ramen noodles between two bowls. Pour the hot broth and vegetables over the noodles.
  7. Garnish with green onions and your favorite toppings. Serve immediately.
