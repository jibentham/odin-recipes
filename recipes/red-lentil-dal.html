Red Lentil Dal

Description

Vegetarian Dal is a nourishing Indian dish featuring red lentils simmered with aromatic spices like turmeric, cumin, and coriander. This creamy, protein-rich stew is enhanced with sautéed onions, garlic, ginger, and fresh tomatoes, creating a flavorful and wholesome meal. Often served with basmati rice or naan, dal is a versatile and satisfying option for lunch or dinner. Its vibrant spices and silky texture make it a comforting and healthful staple in vegetarian cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup red lentils, rinsed
  • 4 cups water or vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon coconut oil or ghee
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2 medium tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • ½ teaspoon red chili powder (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)
  • Optional: Serve with basmati rice or naan

Steps

  1. In a large pot, combine lentils and water/vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer for 20 minutes, skimming off foam as needed.
  2. While lentils cook, heat coconut oil or ghee in a skillet over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds, stirring until they begin to pop.
  3. Add chopped onion, garlic, and ginger, sautéing until golden brown (about 5 minutes).
  4. Stir in turmeric, ground cumin, coriander, and chili powder, cooking for another minute.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft and blended with the spices (about 5 minutes).
  6. Combine the spice mixture with the cooked lentils, adding salt. Simmer for an additional 10 minutes.
  7. Garnish with cilantro and serve with rice or naan.
